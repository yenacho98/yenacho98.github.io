About

I am a PhD student in the Work Stress and Recovery Lab at Virginia Tech. My research focuses on understanding how modern work environments—especially those shaped by digital technology—affect employee well-being, attention, and recovery.

Research Interests

Workplace Breaks & Recovery

Exploring how microbreaks and recovery experiences during the workday influence stress, well-being, and performance.

Social Media & Work

Examining how digital platforms blur work-life boundaries and how feedback, visibility, and online pressure affect well-being.

Self-Regulation & Focus

Investigating how executive function and attention control shape recovery, distraction, and performance in digital work environments.

Projects

Current work includes research on social media breaks during the workday, daily stress and recovery processes, and multilevel analyses of work-life balance and burnout.
